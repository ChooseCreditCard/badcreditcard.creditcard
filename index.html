Bad Credit Card Options

When your credit is in bad shape, the goal is simple: rebuild.

Compare Cards for Bad Credit

What “bad credit” usually means

“Bad credit” often means more than just a low score. Issuers may see:

  • • Multiple late or missed payments
  • • Accounts in collections
  • • Charge-offs or defaults
  • • Very high utilization across cards

That doesn’t mean you’re locked out forever. It just means you need targeted tools.

Card types that still work with bad credit

Secured credit cards

A cash deposit reduces issuer risk and makes approval more likely.

Credit builder cards

Products designed for rebuilding, with clear upgrade paths if you use them well.

Subprime unsecured cards

Higher interest, but still a line of credit if used carefully.

Store & retail cards

Can be easier to get, but should be used sparingly and paid in full.

Rebuild strategy with bad credit

  • • Start with one rebuilding product, not many at once
  • • Pay every bill on time — even one late payment slows progress
  • • Keep usage low: under 30% of your limit, lower if possible
  • • Avoid new non-essential debt while you repair your history

What to expect from issuers

With bad credit, issuers typically offer:

  • • Lower starting limits
  • • Higher APR (interest)
  • • Fewer perks at the beginning
  • • Upgrade or review after 6–24 months of good behavior
